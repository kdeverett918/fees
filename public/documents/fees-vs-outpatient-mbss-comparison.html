Comparison Sheet

Mobile FEES vs Outpatient MBSS

Use this sheet when deciding whether a resident or patient is better served by bedside FEES or an outpatient modified barium swallow study (MBSS / VFSS).

Question Mobile FEES Outpatient MBSS / VFSS
Where does it happen? At bedside, in the facility, physician office, or home when clinically appropriate. In radiology or a hospital-based outpatient setting.
How fast can it happen? Often easier to coordinate because there is no transport or radiology schedule to line up. Can be delayed by radiology availability, transport, and report turnaround.
Radiation? No radiation exposure. Yes. Uses fluoroscopy.
Best visual strengths Secretion management, laryngeal function, pharyngeal residue, repeated reassessment. Oral phase, bolus flow through the swallow, and upper esophageal screening.
Best patient fit Bedbound, isolation, medically fragile, cognitively overwhelmed by transport, or needing rapid bedside answers. Patients who tolerate transport and need broader swallow-phase visualization.
Can usual foods be used? Yes, when clinically appropriate. No. Uses contrast-coated material.
Main limitation Does not visualize the oral or esophageal phase; white-out occurs during the swallow. Not bedside, involves radiation, and may be harder to repeat quickly.

When Mobile FEES Is Often the Better First Move

  • The patient cannot leave the unit safely or easily.
  • The team needs a faster pharyngeal dysphagia answer.
  • The patient may need repeat instrumental reassessment.
  • The care team wants recommendations in the resident's usual eating environment.

When MBSS May Still Be the Better Study

  • The primary question involves oral-stage timing or bolus control.
  • Upper esophageal screening is important.
  • The patient cannot tolerate transnasal endoscopy.
  • The medical team specifically needs radiology-based swallow visualization.
Safe marketing language: FEES is often faster and easier to coordinate than outpatient MBSS when transport and radiology scheduling are barriers. Avoid claiming a universal wait time unless you have your own local data.
