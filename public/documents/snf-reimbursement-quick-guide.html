Facility Guide

SNF Medicare / Medicaid Reimbursement Quick Guide

Reviewed April 2, 2026. Educational workflow guide only. This is not legal advice, payer authorization, or a reimbursement guarantee.

Bottom line: the safest contract model is that the facility pays Mobile FEES LV directly, and the facility decides whether it can recover cost through its own Part A, Part B, managed-care, Medicaid, or private-pay workflow.

1. Start With Resident Status

Resident status Typical payment path Facility action before scheduling
Medicare Part A SNF stay Therapy-related swallowing services are generally treated inside the facility's consolidated billing / PPS structure. Treat the service as facility-paid under the vendor agreement. Do not market FEES as separately reimbursed to the vendor.
Medicare Part B resident in the SNF The facility usually submits the claim under its own billing workflow when services are furnished under arrangement, subject to payer rules. Confirm the building's Part B workflow, therapy enrollment, and documentation expectations before the visit.
Medicare Advantage / managed care Plan-specific. Some buildings bill the plan; some absorb cost under contract if prior authorization or carve-out rules are unclear. Verify the exact plan rule before service. Do not promise reimbursement on the website or in sales copy.
Nevada Medicaid / Medicaid managed care Coverage for FEES should be verified carefully. Public Nevada speech billing guidance is not a reliable basis for broad FEES reimbursement claims. Check the current payer rule or MCO guidance before scheduling. If unclear, treat the service as facility-paid unless a separate financial plan is approved first.
Private-pay SNF resident Usually facility-paid first, then handled by the resident agreement or facility invoice if allowed by the building's policies. Confirm who is financially responsible before the study is booked.

2. The Four-Step Workflow

  1. Confirm the resident's payer category before picking a date.
  2. Obtain the physician or qualified-practitioner order, plus demographics, diagnoses, current diet, and reason instrumental assessment is needed now.
  3. Complete FEES, deliver same-day recommendations, and issue a written report describing airway protection, secretion management, residue, strategies, and diet plan.
  4. Bill through the pre-approved facility pathway and match the invoice to the resident's payer status.

3. How FEES Helps Without Overpromising

FEES can improve diagnostic clarity, support dysphagia documentation, guide safer diet decisions, and help the team justify why an instrumental assessment was necessary. Those are strong operational advantages.

Important: FEES is not itself a PDPM payment trigger. Do not claim that performing FEES automatically increases reimbursement. The better language is that FEES can support timely diagnosis, care planning, and clinically accurate documentation when dysphagia or related SLP conditions are truly present.

4. PDPM-Safe Talking Points

  • Supports timely dysphagia diagnosis and care planning.
  • Can help the chart more accurately reflect swallowing disorder, mechanically altered diet, or SLP-related comorbidities when clinically documented.
  • May reduce delay caused by transport and outpatient radiology scheduling.
  • Helps nursing, therapy, providers, and family act on the same findings quickly.

5. Contract Language Checklist

  • The facility confirms payer status before each study.
  • The facility remains responsible for the vendor invoice unless another pathway is approved in writing.
  • No reimbursement guarantee is made by the FEES provider.
  • Report turnaround, travel radius, cancellation, and after-hours expectations are spelled out.
  • HIPAA and BAA language matches the actual workflow.

Source basis for this guide includes CMS SNF consolidated billing materials, CMS PDPM classification guidance, ASHA's FEES practice portal, and Nevada Medicaid speech billing guidance reviewed on April 2, 2026.
