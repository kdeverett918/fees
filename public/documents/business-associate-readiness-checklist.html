Business Associate Agreement Readiness Checklist

Use this worksheet to decide whether the facility relationship involves business-associate functions and what operational items should be settled before service starts.

1. PHI Flow Review

  • [ ] Will Mobile FEES LV receive referral packets containing PHI?
  • [ ] Will reports or clips be returned to the facility using secure channels?
  • [ ] Will Mobile FEES LV access the facility EHR, portal, or secure upload system?
  • [ ] Will any subcontractor or hosted system handle PHI for this workflow?

2. Operational Decisions

  • [ ] Who is the facility privacy/compliance contact?
  • [ ] What secure transmission method is approved?
  • [ ] What incident-reporting window should the parties follow?
  • [ ] What document-retention expectations apply to shared records?
  • [ ] Are staff allowed to text, or must all communication stay inside approved systems?

3. Items Commonly Confirmed in a BAA

  • Permitted uses and disclosures of PHI
  • Safeguards for privacy and security
  • Breach or security-incident reporting expectations
  • Subcontractor obligations
  • Return or destruction of PHI when the relationship ends, if feasible
HHS provides model BAA language and explains that covered entities generally need appropriate protections in place when contractors perform business-associate functions.
This is not a substitute for your organization's legal review. Use it to prepare for the conversation, not to decide legal sufficiency by itself.
