Patient Consent and Financial Policy

Use for concierge patients or any self-pay pathway requiring written consent and financial acknowledgement.

1. Procedure Description

Fiberoptic Endoscopic Evaluation of Swallowing (FEES) uses a thin flexible scope passed through the nose to allow the clinician to observe throat anatomy, secretion management, and swallowing with food and liquid trials.

2. Potential Benefits

  • Direct visualization of pharyngeal swallowing function.
  • No radiation exposure.
  • Useful for bedside, homebound, or medically fragile patients.
  • Immediate discussion of findings and recommendations.

3. Limitations and Alternatives

  • FEES does not visualize the oral phase or esophageal phase of swallowing.
  • Outpatient MBSS / VFSS may still be recommended when broader visualization is needed.
  • A clinical swallow evaluation without endoscopy may be an alternative in some cases.

4. Risks

  • Brief discomfort during scope placement.
  • Gagging, sneezing, or watering of the eyes.
  • Rare minor nosebleed or vasovagal reaction.
  • In very rare cases, the procedure may need to stop because the patient does not tolerate it.

6. Report and Communication Consent
