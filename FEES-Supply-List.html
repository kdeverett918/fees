3. Leak Testing

Leak testing checks for scope damage that could allow fluid ingress during the HLD soak. It is mandatory before every cleaning cycle per the IFU.

Are Leak Testers Universal?

No. The pump/bulb mechanism is generic (same concept across all brands), but the adapter tip that plugs into the scope’s vent port is brand-specific. Each manufacturer (Olympus, Pentax, Fujifilm, Optim) uses a different connector size and keying on their vent port.

Will an Olympus leak tester (MB-155, ALT-Pro) work on the ENTity XL?
No. The Olympus adapter tip will not physically fit the Optim vent port — different diameter, different keying. Even if you forced a connection, using a non-validated tester violates the Optim IFU and could void your warranty.

Where is the leak test port?
On the handle/proximal body of the scope (since the ENTity XL has no light guide connector, unlike most other scopes). It’s a small capped valve. The exact connector type is in the IFU document that shipped with your scope.

Optim ATT-OP Adapter OEM Required

The only validated adapter tip for the ENTity XL vent port. Connects to any standard leak test pump/bulb body. This is the part that makes the tester Optim-compatible — the pump itself is generic.

Erie Medical Endoscopy.com

Third-Party Universal Testers

Some universal leak testers (Healthmark EndoCheck, STERIS ENDO-LEAK) offer multi-brand adapter kits. Ask the vendor if they have an Optim-compatible tip. The pump body is universal — you only need the correct adapter tip. Call Healthmark (800-521-6224) or STERIS (800-548-4873) to check.

4. Reprocessing Overview

The ENTity XL is a non-channeled scope. This means reprocessing is dramatically simpler than GI endoscopes. No channel brushing, no channel flushing, no forced-air drying of internal lumens. The entire process is external surface cleaning + soak.

Step-by-Step for the ENTity XL

1. Pre-clean (immediately after patient use): Wipe exterior with enzymatic detergent sponge/cloth. Do not let bioburden dry.
2. Leak test: Attach ATT-OP adapter, pressurize, submerge in water, watch for bubbles.
3. Enzymatic wash: Soak scope in enzymatic detergent solution, gently wipe all exterior surfaces.
4. Rinse: Rinse off all detergent residue with clean water.
5. HLD soak: Fully immerse in approved HLD solution for required contact time (see chemical table below).
6. Rinse: 1–3 rinses with clean/sterile water (per HLD chemical IFU).
7. Dry & store: Air dry or gently dry with lint-free cloth. Store in clean case or ventilated cabinet.

7. Simplest Method: Disposable Scope Sheaths

No HLD Required — Sheaths Eliminate Chemical Soaking

The FDA recognizes three compliant reprocessing pathways for nasopharyngoscopes. One of them is: disposable sheath + low-level disinfection only. No HLD soak. No chemicals. No basins.

  • A sterile polyurethane sheath covers the scope — it never touches the patient directly
  • After the exam, remove and discard the sheath
  • Wipe scope with enzymatic detergent + 70% isopropyl alcohol wipe
  • That’s it. Ready for the next patient.

BR Surgical Flexible Rhinolaryngoscope Sheath FDA-Cleared Class II

Size: Fits 3.4–3.6 mm scopes (matches ENTity XL exactly)
Material: Sterile disposable polyurethane
FDA status: Class II medical device, specifically cleared for nasopharyngoscopes
Clinical evidence: Two peer-reviewed clinical trials validate sheath + LLD as microbiologically equivalent to HLD
Cost: ~$8–15 per sheath (box of 20)

Caveat: Some facilities may still require HLD even with sheaths (concern about micro-perforations). Verify with each facility’s infection control policies and with the Nevada state board.

11. Mobile Kit Checklist

Run through this before leaving for every facility visit.

⚠️ Important Compliance Notes

• Always follow your scope’s IFU (Instructions for Use) — the document that shipped with the ENTity XL, or request from Optim.
• Follow BOTH the scope IFU AND the HLD chemical IFU.
• Document every reprocessing cycle in a log (date, time, chemical lot, test strip result).
• Verify each facility’s infection control policies — some may require HLD even when using sheaths.
• Nevada NRS 637B authorizes SLPs to perform flexible nasal endoscopy for swallowing evaluation.
• Your compliance obligations: (1) Scope manufacturer IFU, (2) Chemical manufacturer IFU, (3) CDC/FDA guidelines, (4) Facility infection control policies.
